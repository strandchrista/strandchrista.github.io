The challenge

Why innovation struggles in construction and real estate.

Innovation in the built environment rarely fails because of technology alone. More often, it fails because the path to adoption is unclear. Construction and real estate projects involve layered decision-making, fragmented accountability, and stakeholders who evaluate value through very different lenses.

The person using a product is often not the one paying for it. Developers, consultants, contractors, owners, and investors each assess risk differently, and even strong solutions can lose momentum if they are not positioned within the realities of procurement, capital planning, and project execution.
